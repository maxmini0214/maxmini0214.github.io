❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I remove line breaks from text?
Paste your text and click "Remove Line Breaks". It replaces all newline characters with spaces. In code: text.replace(/\n/g, ' ') (JS) or text.replace('\n', ' ') (Python) or tr '\n' ' ' < file.txt (Bash).
How do I count characters and words?
Stats update in real-time as you type. Note: bytes ≠ characters for Unicode — emoji = 4 bytes, CJK characters = 3 bytes in UTF-8. In JS: new Blob([text]).size for bytes, text.trim().split(/\s+/).length for words.
What's the difference between URL encode and HTML escape?
URL encoding: spaces → %20, & → %26 (for URLs). Uses encodeURIComponent(). HTML escaping: < → &lt;, > → &gt; (prevents XSS). Different purposes: URL encoding for query strings, HTML escaping for safe rendering.
Can I share my text with others?
Yes! Click 🔗 Share to encode your text into a URL. The recipient sees the same text automatically. Everything stays in the URL — nothing sent to a server. Note: very long texts may exceed URL limits (~2000 chars).
Is my text data safe?
100% safe. Everything runs in your browser — no data is sent to any server. No cookies, no analytics on your content, no server-side processing. Your text never leaves your machine.
What are command-line alternatives?
Bash: sed 's/^ *//;s/ *$//' (trim), sort -u (dedupe+sort), wc -l (lines), wc -w (words), tr '[:lower:]' '[:upper:]' (uppercase). Python: str.strip(), textwrap.dedent(). This tool is for quick one-off ops without a terminal.
