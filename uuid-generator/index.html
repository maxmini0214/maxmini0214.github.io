❓ FAQ

What is a UUID?

A UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit identifier that is practically guaranteed to be unique across all devices and time. UUID v4 uses random numbers, making collisions virtually impossible (1 in 2^122).

Is UUID v4 truly unique?

Yes, for all practical purposes. The probability of generating two identical UUID v4s is about 1 in 5.3 × 10^36. You'd need to generate 1 billion UUIDs per second for 85 years to have a 50% chance of one collision.

UUID v4 vs v1 — what's the difference?

UUID v1 uses your MAC address and timestamp, which can leak device info. UUID v4 is purely random — no identifiable information. V4 is preferred for privacy and simplicity.

Is this tool free?

Yes, completely free. No signup, no ads, no usage limits. Runs 100% in your browser — no data is sent to any server.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Once loaded, it works entirely offline using your browser's built-in crypto.randomUUID() function.

What format are the UUIDs?

Standard UUID v4: xxxxxxxx-xxxx-4xxx-yxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx. Toggle hyphens on/off and uppercase/lowercase with the checkboxes above.
