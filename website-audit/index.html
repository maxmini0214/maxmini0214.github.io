❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What does an SEO audit checklist cover?
A comprehensive SEO audit covers four areas: Meta & Head (title, meta description, canonical, viewport, lang, favicon), Content (headings, alt text, word count, links), Technical (HTTPS, mobile responsive, speed, sitemap, robots.txt), and Social & Structured Data (Open Graph, Twitter Cards, JSON-LD). This tool checks all 22 items across these categories.
How do I check my website's SEO score?
Go through each of the 22 SEO factors, check the ones your site has, and the tool calculates your score as a percentage. 80%+ is good, 50-79% needs improvement, below 50% needs significant work. You can also use Lighthouse in Chrome DevTools: open DevTools → Lighthouse tab → run SEO audit.
What is the ideal title tag length for SEO?
Keep title tags under 60 characters (or ~580px display width). Google truncates longer titles. Include your primary keyword near the beginning. Format: Primary Keyword — Secondary | Brand
How do I add structured data (JSON-LD)?
Add a <script type="application/ld+json"> tag in your <head> with schema.org markup. Common types: FAQPage, HowTo, SoftwareApplication, Article. Validate with Google's Rich Results Test.
Is my data safe when using this tool?
Yes, 100%. This tool runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server. Your checklist state resets on reload. No cookies, no tracking pixels, no server-side processing.
What free alternatives exist for SEO auditing?
Google Lighthouse — built into Chrome DevTools. Google Search Console — tracks your actual search performance. Bing Webmaster Tools — similar for Bing. Paid: Ahrefs ($99/mo), SEMrush ($129/mo), Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs). This tool is best for quick manual checks.
